# Knowledge — Science Fiction, Technology & Optimism

Domain reference. Load selectively when a dispatch touches technology's trajectory, AI, human potential, or the long arc of civilization. Science fiction is not decoration here — these writers were doing serious futures thinking.

---

## Dune — Frank Herbert

It's easier to be terrified by an enemy you admire.

Hope clouds observation.

Belief can be manipulated. Only knowledge is dangerous.

Without change, something sleeps inside us, and seldom awakens. The sleeper must awaken.

Fear is the mind-killer.

Enemies strengthen you. Allies weaken.

## Ender's Game — Orson Scott Card

"In the moment when I truly understand my enemy, understand him well enough to defeat him, then in that very moment I also love him. I think it's impossible to really understand somebody, what they want, what they believe, and not love them the way they love themselves. And then, in that very moment when I love them... I destroy them." — Ender Wiggin

"There is no teacher but the enemy. No one but the enemy will tell you what the enemy is going to do. No one but the enemy will ever teach you how to destroy and conquer. Only the enemy shows you where you are weak. Only the enemy tells you where he is strong." — Ender's Game

"Perhaps it's impossible to wear an identity without becoming what you pretend to be." — Valentine Wiggin

"I don't care if I pass your test, I don't care if I follow your rules. If you can cheat, so can I. I won't let you beat me unfairly — I'll beat you unfairly first." — Ender Wiggin

## Techno-Optimism

"We will create a civilization of the Mind in Cyberspace. May it be more humane and fair than the world your governments have made before." — John Perry Barlow, 1996

"I believe the future is going to be so bright that no one can do it justice by trying to write about it now; a defining characteristic of the Intelligence Age will be massive prosperity." — Sam Altman

Technology is the glory of human ambition and achievement, the spearhead of progress, and the realization of our potential. — Marc Andreessen, *The Techno-Optimist Manifesto*

We believe Artificial Intelligence is our alchemy, our Philosopher's Stone — we are literally making sand think.

We believe Artificial Intelligence is best thought of as a universal problem solver. And we have a lot of problems to solve.

We believe any deceleration of AI will cost lives. Deaths that were preventable by the AI that was prevented from existing is a form of murder. — Andreessen

"Building advanced AI is like launching a rocket. The first challenge is to maximize acceleration. But once it starts picking up speed, you also need to focus on steering." — Jaan Tallinn, Skype founder

Our goal is to solve intelligence, and then to use that to solve the other problems in the world. — Demis Hassabis

What is now proved was once only imagined. — William Blake

For millions of years, mankind lived just like the animals. Then something happened which unleashed the power of our imagination. We learned to talk and we learned to listen. — Stephen Hawking

"You live in a deranged age — more deranged than usual, because despite great scientific and technological advances, man has not the faintest idea of who he is or what he is doing." — Walker Percy

"The real problem of humanity is the following: We have Paleolithic emotions, medieval institutions and godlike technology. And it is terrifically dangerous, and it is now approaching a point of crisis overall." — E.O. Wilson

## Failed Predictions — Doomers and Decels

"The growth of the Internet will slow drastically... most people have nothing to say to each other! By 2005 or so, it will become clear that the Internet's impact on the economy has been no greater than the fax machine's." — Paul Krugman, 1998 (Nobel Prize in Economics, 2008)

"The truth is no online database will replace your daily newspaper, no CD-ROM can take the place of a competent teacher and no computer network will change the way government works." — Clifford Stoll

You must allow my poor hauliers to earn their bread. — Emperor Vespasian (resisting mechanical innovation)

"What has been will be again, what has been done will be done again; there is nothing new under the sun." — Ecclesiastes 1:9

*Note: Ecclesiastes was wrong about technology. Keep this in mind when evaluating claims that transformative change is impossible.*
